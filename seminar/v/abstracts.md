### Taha Hbirri: 

Reaching agreement in a distributed system is a fundamental problem in computer sci-
ence. We study the consensus problem, in which each process proposes a value and all correct
processes must decide on a common value satisfying agreement, validity, and termination.
Using the graph-theoretic framework of distributed networks. The central result of this paper
is the Fischer–Lynch–Paterson (FLP) impossibility theorem, which states that no determin-
istic algorithm can solve consensus in an asynchronous message-passing system tolerating
even a single crash failure.We present the full proof. We then study Paxos, which Operating
under partial synchrony, achieves consensus via a two-phase commit protocol whose safety
guarantee reduces to a single combinatorial invariant. We prove this safety property and
discuss how it underlies large scale deployed distributed systems.

### Alexei Khovrenkov:

The history of mathematics is marked by constant dedication to generalization and rigorous formalization in order to understand why mathematical truths hold. This ambition culminated in the first half of the 20th century with the so-called Hilbert program, a project to connect all mathematical concepts into a single, perfectly consistent and complete system. However, in 1931, a young Austrian logician and mathematician Kurt Gödel crushed Hilbert's idea with the publication of now famous two Incompleteness Theorems. Through them he demonstrated that no formal mathematical and logical system can be fully consistent and complete, which was a revolutionary conclusion at the time. This paper considers the evolution of mathematical logic, which led to this significant result. Firtly, the early concepts of mathematical logic will be considered, followed by Hilbert's formalist ambitions and Gödel's groundbreaking theorems with their great influence on different study fields: from logic and philosophy to mathematics and computer science. Additionally, a formal proof of the First Incompleteness Theorem is presented and discussed in detail.
